# First Meeting Tips

## Neutral territory
Always arrange first meetings on neutral ground — a park or open field, never
at either dog's home. Home dogs may feel territorial and react defensively.

## Both dogs on leash
Keep both dogs on a loose leash for the first introduction. A tight leash
signals tension to the dog and can trigger reactivity.

## Read body language
Signs of comfort: relaxed tail wag, soft eyes, play bow.
Signs of stress: stiff body, tucked tail, whale eye, growling.
If stress signals appear, calmly separate the dogs and try again another day.

## Size difference
When there is a significant size difference, keep the meeting short (5-10 minutes)
and ensure the smaller dog has an escape route.

## Age difference
Puppies and elderly dogs need special care. Puppies may overwhelm senior dogs —
limit sessions to 10 minutes and supervise closely.
